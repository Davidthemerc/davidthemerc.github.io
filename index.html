About me

The road that brought me here to web development in 2022 starts a quite a few years back, during my high school years at Edison High School in southwest Fresno. I was coming off a freshman year where I had already been put on academic probation. I just never really felt engaged with any of the classes I had that year and never felt compelled to give my best effort. That changed a bit during my sophomore year, when I had the opportunity to take a class on web design.

It wasn’t really a particularly deep class, now that I think about it, but it was fun, and that made me really apply myself and give my full effort. We learned how to write web pages in HTML using Notepad (and later Microsoft FrontPage) and the teacher would lecture about various topics about the Internet and the Web. It made me consider a career in Web Development, but my continued struggles in other classes caused me to shy away from that idea.

After high school, I went to Fresno City College with the goal of eventually transferring to Fresno State. My first priority was to get all of my general education out of the way and with good grades, so the whole web development idea took a backseat and I just never really returned to it. I eventually transferred to Fresno State and graduated with a bachelor’s degree in Political Science (that’s a story for another day).

I’ve been toying with the idea of going back to school to learn web development and programming for a few years now, and when the pandemic hit and online classes became the norm, I seized my opportunity. I definitely want to do that now while I'm still fairly young and able to produce at a high level. I may have successfully graduated from college and earned my bachelor's, but I felt like I left a lot of work unfinished when I didn't take any computer courses.

Since I'm in a secure place with my career right now, I want to push hard and come back and take all the courses that I should have taken while I was still in school. I may focus heavily on web development, but if there are other classes that interest me, such as Python, you can bet I will take a serious look at them in my quest to obtain all the knowledge that I felt I missed out on in my college years. It's the best decision for my personal growth, and for potential future career growth.

And so, here I am today!
