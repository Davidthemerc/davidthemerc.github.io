Teriyaki Don

Teriyaki Don

My dad and I were out on the town doing something (probably shopping at his favorite store, the 99 Cents Only Store, I can't really say for sure), when we decided that we were going to eat out before we went home. He had heard about this teriyaki restaurant, Teriyaki Don, from a coworker who recommended it to him. It sounds good to me, so we drove over to Cedar & Herndon where it's located. They specialize in donburis, which are Japanese-style rice bowl dishes, which at Teriyaki Don, consist of rice, a small salad, and your choice of meat. They have quite a few choices of donburi, but I usually stick to two: the NY Steak Donburi (medium rare), and the Spicy Crispy Chicken Donburi.

My Favorite Donburis at Teriyaki Don

NY Steak Don

The NY Steak Don is my go-to choice. It's very similar to the Beef Don, except that the Beef Don uses a different cut of meat, and you can't choose the cooking temperature for it. You can choose the temperature for the NY Steak; as a general rule I always go with medium rare. Not too charred, but not blue or too rare. The NY Steak strips always come out nice and juicy when done medium rare.

Spicy Crispy Chicken Don

The Spicy Crispy Chicken Don is a fairly recent second choice for me. I've typically gone with the regular Chicken Don for most of my time patronizing Teriyaki Don, but my girlfriend introduced me to the Spicy Crispy Chicken Don, and I've never really gone back to the Chicken Don since (it seems so, frankly plain, in comparison!) The crispy breading is excellent, and it's seasoned just right. On occasion, even if I do other a NY Steak Don, I will still order a side of Spicy Crispy Chicken. I think it's that good!
