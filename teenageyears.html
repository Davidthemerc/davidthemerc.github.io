My Teenage Years

My teenage years were a good bit more difficult than my early years. I entered my teenage years going to school at Edison High School in West Fresno, having moved up from Computech Middle School. I had showed signs of struggling in school throughout middle school, including failing Algebra 1. As a result, I had to take the class again during my freshman year. To give you an idea of how much I struggled with math during these years, I'll let you know a little secret of mine. I actually failed Algebra 1 again, forcing me to take it again my sophomore year. Very fun! Luckily, math was one of the few subjects that I really struggled with. I was above-average in English, History, average with Biology and the other sciences, and a bit below average in other, less interesting subjects (such as Sociology.)

I hated Algebra, but you would think I loved it, considering I took it: 

In general, I never really felt engaged with any of my classes during high school. One of the few exceptions was my web design class in my sophomore year, which I mention in the Index page. Of course, high school being high school, there was always something wrong that you could hate even more than your classes. For me, my greatest hatred about high school was not classes, not bullies, and not the draconian controls on our freedoms that the school occasionally imposed upon students (for instance, during a particularly bad wave of fights at school, we weren't allowed to walk in groups larger than 3!) It was, in fact, the lunch rush. If your third period class wasn't located near the Cafeteria, and you weren't a world class sprinter, you didn't stand a snowball's chance in hell of making it to the lunch line before half the student body did. Most of the time, I did not even bother running to the lunch line, because I'd never make it in time to beat the rush regardless! My third period classes were never near the cafeteria! Sigh...

Long lines to get food are the worst!
