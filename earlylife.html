My Early Life

My Early Life

I was born in Downey, California (a suburb of Los Angeles) about one score and some change ago. I went to Rio Hondo Elementary school for kindergarten through fifth grade. The six years I spent there were the longest I ever spent at any single school. We had annual water ballon fights in 2nd and 3rd grade, which were upgraded to water gun fights in 4th and 5th grade. Looking back, I had a lot of fun in those years, we really used our imaginations to the limit in those early years. There was one day, in 4th grade, when a swarm of bees accidentally found its way onto the playground, and everybody ran like hell to get away. Later that day, a group of us met up at afternoon recess to "investigate" where the bees came from. Somebody said they were Mexican killer bees, which apparently was a big scare at the time. We didn't really have any way of knowing, so we all just sort of accepted that thought. We later found the bees over by the school fence, which separated the school from the park, and told the school where they were. The bees disappeared after that; they probably either flew away, or maybe the school called somebody to come pick them up. Either way, it was a pretty interesting memory.

My dad used to take me with him to work on the weekends, which was really a side job of stocking and maintaining all of the vending machines that his bosses let him keep there. He worked at several garment factories, and the vending machines were mainly used by the garment workers. He used to keep a steady supply of sodas in snacks in my closet, which we would take with us on the weekends to resupply the machines. I became an older brother at six years old, and mercifully only ended up having one brother. One was enough!

The TRW Amateur Radio/Computer Swap Meet in Redondo Beach, CA.

My favorite thing to do with my dad was to visit the computer swap meets that were held once or twice a month in Redondo Beach and Santa Ana. Since I was 5, we'd always had a desktop computer at home. As I got older, my dad and I started going to these swap meets, and we'd go around the entire swap meet, buying various Macintosh parts until I eventually had an entire system. I went through several different systems over the years, upgrading at least once as year as better computer components started showing up at the swap meet. It was during these times that my love of computers was formed.

Some of the Macintosh computers I used to have

Just finding the right parts for these was a scavenger hunt!

Macintosh Plus

We found this gem at a Goodwill, although we had to really dig around piles and piles of stuff to find the mouse and keyboard that worked with this thing! The connectors resemble phone line connectors more than your typical computer cable connection! I wish I knew what happened to it!

Power Macintosh 6100

The first computer I owned that had a built-in CD-ROM drive! I had an older AppleCD external drive on a previous Mac (one I can't even remember anymore or I'd list it!), but it used a CD caddy (annoying to load and unload CDs), was much slower, and as I recall, did not work 100% of the time. Having a good CD-ROM drive was a must, as all the good games were on CD-ROM!
